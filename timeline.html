Timeline

Milestones and highlights from J-PAL Africa's first decade

2020

  • J-PAL Africa turns 10!

    J-PAL Africa turns 10! Sign up for our newsletter to stay up to date: j-p.al/news

2019

  • Tackling youth unemployment

    The policy team holds trainings with career counsellors in the Western Cape and Gauteng, as well as with NGOs, on the evidence based action plan and reference letter interventions

    POLICY

  • DigiFI Africa launch

    J-PAL Africa launches the Digital Identification and Finance Research Initiative (DigiFI), to study the impact of DigiFI initiatives on citizens and governments across Africa Eight research projects funded in the first round Request for Proposals (RFP)

    RESEARCH

  • TaRL Africa launch

    J-PAL and Pratham create a new joint venture, Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL) Africa to support partners working to address the learning crisis in primary schools in Africa through the evidence-based TaRL approach.

    POLICY

2018

  • TaRL expansion in Africa

    The Borno State Government launches a TaRL pilot in Borno, Nigeria

    Cote d’Ivoire’s Ministry of National Education launches a TaRL pilot, le Programme d’Enseignment Ciblé (PEC)

    POLICY

2017

  • Sierra Leone evidence-based policy adoptions

    J-PAL Africa team collaboratively supports a pilot intervention with the World Food Programme based on incentives for immunisation research

    J-PAL Africa supports Search for Common ground as they expand parliamentary debates

    POLICY

2016

  • Evaluating labour market interventions

    Collaboration with the South African Department of Labour to evaluate the role of reference letters and skill accreditation in the South African labour market and a separate evaluation with the World Bank and Department of Labour on the impact of job search planning.

    Collaboration with Harambee Youth Employment Accelerator to evaluate the impact of providing information about jobseekers’ skills on employment in South Africa.

    RESEARCH

  • TaRL pilot launches in Zambia

    The Ministry of General Education in Zambia launches adapted Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL) pilot, Catch Up, in 80 schools

    POLICY

2015

  • Evaluating the impact of prepaid electricity

    Collaboration with City of Cape Town to study the impact of prepaid electricity meters on electricity use and utility revenue

    RESEARCH

  • National School Deworming

    Launch of National School Deworming Programme in South Africa under the Department of Basic Education’s Integrated School Health Programme. The programme was a joint initiative with Departments Health and Social Development supported by evidence and guidance from J-PAL Africa and Evidence Action.

    POLICY

2014

  • Project Incubation Fund launch

    J-PAL Africa launches the Project Incubation Fund to support researchers carrying out exploratory policy-relevant research questions in South Africa. The first round awarded grants to studies looking at maternal and child health, WASH interventions, prepaid electricity meters and young voter motivation.

    RESEARCH

2013

  • J-PAL Africa's first policy event

    J-PAL co-hosts the Decision Science Symposium with the Government of Rwanda in Kigali, to discuss key development priorities and how to use impact evaluations to inform policy. Over 80 government officials attend.

    Following this event, the Rwanda Agricultural Board, with J-PAL Africa support, launches a Water Tanks Loan Programme pilot based on evidence from a randomised evaluation conducted in Kenya

    POLICY

2012

  • Evaluating a financial literacy programme

    Collaboration with Old Mutual, the Russia Financial Literacy and Education Fund, and the Women’s Development Business to evaluate the effectiveness of a financial literacy programme

    RESEARCH

2011

  • Evaluating Social Programmes course

    J-PAL’s flagship Executive Education Course -- Evaluating Social Programmes runs for the first year in Africa hosted in Cape Town with over 80 participants

2010

  • J-PAL Africa office opens!

    J-PAL Africa office opens in August 2010 within the Southern African Labour and Research Unit at the University of Cape Town’s (UCT) School of Economics. J-PAL Africa hosts a launch conference in 2011
